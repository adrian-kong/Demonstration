Integral Approximation

This approximates the area under the given function through two seperate techniques that are
currently included. One of which is the Riemann Sum which approximates the area by the summation
of rectangles and the other is trapezoidal rule which similarly, approximates via the summation
of trapezoids under the curve.

Riemann sum

=========================================================
Quick Summary
Riemann Sum is basically creating rectangles under the function and summing the area to
approximate the total area under the curve within the given domain. This method however
consists of many variation. I.e. to reduce error bounds, it is possible to calculate
a Riemann Sum of a function from left to right and vice versa. It can also be calculated
by taking the area from the midpoints of every partition. Nonetheless, these errors
could be minimized by increasing the quantity of partitions (which are the amounts
of rectangles under the curve) and easily approximate the area under the curve.
